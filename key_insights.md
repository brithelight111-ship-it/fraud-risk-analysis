## 1. High-Value Transactions Carry Disproportionate Fraud Risk
Transactions in the highest amount bucket show significantly higher fraud rates compared to lower-value transactions, despite lower volume.

**Impact:** Monitoring rules should prioritize high-value transactions to reduce financial exposure.

## 2. Risk Scores Effectively Differentiate Fraudulent Behavior
Transactions labeled as high risk based on combined device and IP risk scores have materially higher fraud rates than low-risk transactions.

**Impact:** Risk scoring is an effective signal for triggering enhanced verification or review.

## 3. Fraud Activity Varies by Transaction Type
Certain transaction types (e.g., POS and online transactions) show elevated fraud rates relative to others.

**Impact:** Channel-specific controls may reduce fraud without impacting legitimate users.

## 4. Time-Based Patterns Indicate Elevated Risk During Off-Hours
Fraud incidents are more frequent during early-morning hours.

**Impact:** Time-based rules can be used to strengthen fraud detection during high-risk periods.
